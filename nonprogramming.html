Emerging Technologies

Reflection

I chose to focus on RFID as an emerging technology for this assignment. Through my research, I was able to learn how RFID is used today, how it works, its effectiveness, and how it could be used in the future. One example of RFID today is Tap-To-Pay on bank cards. I still have more to learn about RFID, such as how to apply this technology today.

Responses

Question One

Question - If your emerging technology were fully realized today, how might life be different?

Answer - RFID would make life much more convinient and efficient. People will no longer have to stop at checkpoints such as store checkout, airport security, or border patrol, and instead can walk through and have the RFID system automatically identify them and go through with the required process. While privacy may be an issue, many people would enjoy the efficiency brought along by this technology.

Question Two

Question - If you were sent back in time 1000 years, how might you explain your emerging technology to the locals?

I would explain RFID as a living being that has hyper-senses, allowing it to detect things the locals might find useful at the time. They could use this 'being' to find out when vulnurable products such as dairy or meats have spoiled without having to check themselves.

Question Three

Question - How might future technology not negatively impact the natural environment?

Answer - RFID itself cannot negativley impact the environment, due to how it works. RFID is simply a way of transferring information wirelessly. The production process of RFID could harm the environment by releasing greenhouse gasses, but that is simply the fault of the industrial process and not the technology.
